Programs Offered:


  • Bachelor of Social Work(B.S.W.)

The Department of Social Work is an essential part of the academic sector that provides students with the knowledge and skills to promote social change, solve problems in human relationships, and empower individuals and communities. This department typically offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs that cover a wide range of topics related to social work, such as social welfare policy, human behavior in the social environment, community organizing, and social justice.

The curriculum of the Department of Social Work is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the social, political, and economic factors that influence individual and group behavior. Students learn about various social problems, such as poverty, discrimination, and violence, and gain practical skills to help individuals, families, and communities overcome these challenges.

The Department of Social Work is often closely linked with social service agencies and organizations, and many academic programs in this department have strong community connections. This means that students may have access to internships, field placements, and volunteer opportunities that allow them to gain practical experience and make a positive impact in their communities.
